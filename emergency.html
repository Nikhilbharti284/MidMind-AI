Emergency Help is Here

Immediate assistance when you need it most.

Police

Emergency Number

The police are the front-line force responsible for maintaining law and order. In any critical situation involving crime, violence, or disturbance of public peace, you should contact the police immediately. When you dial 100, you will be connected to trained professionals who will gather essential information about your location and the nature of the emergency. They are equipped with the latest technology, including body cameras and real-time tracking systems, ensuring rapid response.

Police services also engage in community outreach and education to help citizens understand how to stay safe. They offer guidance on how to secure your home, report suspicious activities, and interact with law enforcement in emergencies. It is important to remain calm during an emergency call—provide clear, concise details and follow the operator's instructions.

Ambulance

Medical Emergency

Ambulance services are vital in medical emergencies, providing rapid transport and on-scene care. When you call 108, a professional dispatcher will assess the situation and send a well-equipped ambulance with paramedics or EMTs. These teams are trained in emergency medical procedures, such as CPR and advanced life support, to stabilize patients before hospital arrival.

During the call, remain calm and provide as many details as possible—this includes your location, the patient’s condition, and any visible injuries or symptoms. Ambulance teams are also supported by modern GPS tracking and communication tools, ensuring that help reaches you promptly.

Fire Department

Fire Emergency

The fire department is dedicated to saving lives and property in the event of a fire. When you call 101, trained firefighters and emergency response teams are dispatched to control the blaze, rescue occupants, and prevent further damage. They use specialized equipment like fire hoses, breathing apparatus, and thermal imaging cameras to manage hazardous situations.

In addition to extinguishing fires, the fire department also provides guidance on fire prevention and safety, including evacuation procedures, use of smoke detectors, and creating fire escape plans. Always prioritize your safety and that of others by evacuating immediately if a fire occurs.

Emergency Guide

Step 1: Stay Calm

In any emergency, the first step is to remain calm. When panic sets in, your judgment and ability to act quickly can be severely impaired. Take deep, steady breaths to help slow your heart rate and focus your mind. By staying calm, you can more clearly assess the situation and determine the best course of action. Techniques like mindfulness, deep breathing, or even simply pausing for a moment to collect your thoughts can make a significant difference.

Remaining calm also ensures that you can communicate effectively with emergency responders. They need clear, accurate information to dispatch the appropriate assistance. Remember, your calmness can have a positive impact on others around you—helping to keep the overall situation under control.

Step 2: Call Emergency Services

Once you are composed, dial the appropriate emergency number immediately. For criminal activities, call the police; for medical emergencies, contact ambulance services; and for fire emergencies, reach out to the fire department. When you make the call, speak clearly and provide the dispatcher with essential details such as your exact location, the nature of the emergency, and any relevant observations.

It is important to stay on the line and answer any follow-up questions the operator may have. This information will help them prioritize your call and dispatch the correct type of emergency response. Having a clear plan and knowing the right number to call in each situation can save valuable time and potentially lives.

Step 3: Follow Instructions

After contacting emergency services, carefully listen to the instructions provided by the operator. These instructions are designed to ensure your safety and the safety of those around you. Whether you are advised to evacuate the area, administer basic first aid, or simply wait for emergency responders, following these directions is critical.

If you are with others, coordinate with them and assign roles if possible—for example, one person may be responsible for keeping calm while another provides additional details to the dispatcher. Trust that the emergency protocols are designed to mitigate risk, and avoid taking actions that could exacerbate the situation. Your adherence to these guidelines will help ensure a quicker, more effective response.
